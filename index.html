Description/goals
When we think of data stories, we often think of charts and infographics, but in reality a significant number of stories from daily reports to months-long investigative pieces rely heavily on acquiring, cleaning, searching and understanding large datasets. The goal of this class is to get you familiar with some tools that will allow you to tell a better story with data.

Homework grading scale
A
Piece is ready for publication
B
Piece needs moderate copyediting and/or structure reconsideration
C
Structure and content of entire piece needs to be rethought and/or has significant reporting gaps
D
Piece is underreported and poorly structured
F
Piece is not relevant to the assignment
Late homework experiences exponential grade decay
  • Homework that is one day late loses one point
  • Homework that is two days late loses an additional two points
  • Homework that is three days late loses an additional four points
  • Homework that is four days late loses an additional eight points
  • Etc
Collaboration
Students are encouraged to discuss assignments. For the most part, you will be working on different things, so talking about your work should not be a problem. Whiteboarding your way through a sticky problem with a friend or looking online for answers to technical questions are encouraged (programmers do that all the time!) Your final work, however, must be entirely your own. A good rule of thumb is that no one else should take over your keyboard while completing an assignment.

Final Project
Put one publication-ready piece of data-backed journalism online.
